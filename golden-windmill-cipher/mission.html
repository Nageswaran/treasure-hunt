Internet Detective Academy · Case File

The Golden Windmill Cipher

Agent briefing — read carefully before you begin
⏱ Estimated case time: 50-70 minutes

During restoration work on an old Dutch windmill, a worker found a sealed brass tube hidden inside the cap. Inside was a scrap of coded paper and half a map — nothing else. The mill's owners have called in the Internet Detective Academy. Somewhere nearby, a small chest has been waiting almost three centuries to be found. Crack every clue, and you'll be the one to open it.

MISSION 01
EASY

How Many Giants Stand Guard?

The map fragment shows a cluster of windmills next to a river, but no name. Search online for the famous group of windmills at Kinderdijk in the Netherlands, a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Find out exactly how many windmills stand together there.

Hint 1

Try searching the official Kinderdijk website or its Wikipedia page — look for the total count of windmills at the site.

💡 Did you know? The Kinderdijk windmills weren't built to grind grain at all. They were built to pump water out of the low-lying polder, so the land behind the dikes would stay dry.

MISSION 02
EASY

Count the Sails

A sketch of the windmill was tucked inside the tube too. Count the sails (the big spinning arms) drawn on it — a traditional Dutch windmill always has the same number.

Look closely — how many separate sails spin around the centre hub?
Hint 1

Every traditional Dutch windmill has the same number of sails. Count them in pairs across from each other.

MISSION 03
MEDIUM

The Miller's Word List

Tucked in the tube was a torn page from the old miller's Dutch notebook, listing five windmill words. Four of them use "de" and one uses "het". Find the odd one out.

molen · wiek · dijk · kaart · dorp

Hint 1

Search "de of het" plus each word to check its article. Four words share the same one.

MISSION 04
MEDIUM

The Grain Room Floor

Below the mill's turning gears is an L-shaped grain-storage room. The old floor plan gives you the outer measurements and the size of a missing corner. Find the total floor area in square metres.

The full floor would be a rectangle, but a corner is missing. Split it into two rectangles to work out the total area.
Hint 1

Imagine the whole shape as one big rectangle, then subtract the missing corner's area.

💡 Did you know? The name Kinderdijk means "Children's Dike." Legend says the name comes from a great flood in 1421, when people found a baby safe in a floating cradle, kept balanced by a cat pacing back and forth to stop it from tipping over.

MISSION 05
HARD

The Shifted Letters

The coded strip from the brass tube uses a simple trick: every letter was shifted forward by 3 in the alphabet (A→D, B→E, C→F, and so on, wrapping back to A after Z). Shift each letter back by 3 to read the message. It names the group of eight mills you must investigate next.

QHGHUZDDUG

Hint 1

Write out the alphabet and mark every 3rd letter back, or just count backwards 3 letters from each one in the code.

Hint 2

The first letter Q shifted back 3 is N. Keep going letter by letter.

MISSION 06
HARD

Four Millers, One Secret

Four millers — Anna, Bram, Cato, and Daan — each tend one of mills No. 1, No. 3, No. 5, and No. 7 in the Nederwaard group. Use only these clues to work out who tends which mill:

1. Cato's mill number is the largest of the four.
2. Daan's mill is number 3.
3. Bram's mill is not number 5.
4. Anna's mill number is greater than Bram's.

The treasure is hidden at the mill of whichever miller's name comes first in the alphabet. Which mill number is it?

Hint 1

Start with the clue that gives you an exact number right away, then work out who's left for the remaining three mills.

Hint 2

Once Daan's mill is fixed, only three numbers remain for Anna, Bram and Cato. Clue 1 tells you the biggest one right away.

💡 Did you know? The eight stone Nederwaard windmills were built in 1738 — and just two years later, in 1740, eight wooden Overwaard windmills were added nearby to help pump even more water.

MISSION 07
BIG AHA!

The Combination Lock

Under the miller's floorboards is a small chest with a 4-dial combination lock. Build the code from what you've already discovered, in this order:

Dial 1-2: the number of windmills at Kinderdijk (Mission 1).
Dial 3: the mill number where the treasure hides (Mission 6).
Dial 4: the number of sails on a Dutch windmill (Mission 2).

Put the digits together, in order, with no spaces. What's the full code?

Hint 1

Line up your three earlier answers side by side, in the exact order listed above, and squeeze them into one number.

GRAND FINALE
FINALE

What the Chest Was Really Guarding

The chest creaks open. Inside is one last note: a row of numbers. Use A=1, B=2, C=3, all the way to Z=26, to turn each number into a letter and spell out one word.

4 - 9 - 19 - 3 - 15 - 22 - 5 - 18 - 25

Hint 1

Write the alphabet out with a number under each letter, then match each number in the code to its letter.

Hint 2

The first number, 4, is the letter D. The word has 9 letters in total.

Case closed?

The chest never held gold at all — just the miller's old notebook, full of questions he never got to answer. The real treasure was the chase itself: every windmill, cipher, and clue along the way was its own small discovery. Well done, Agent.
