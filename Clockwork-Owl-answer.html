🕵️ Answer Key: The Mystery of the Clockwork Owl

This file contains the exact solutions and explanations for all 7 missions. Do not let the child see this document!

Mission 1: The Dutch Clockmaker
Answer: C
Proof: A Google search for "who invented the pendulum clock in 1656" returns Christiaan Huygens. The first letter of his first name is C.
Mission 2: The Owl's Base
Answer: O
Proof: In the Dutch language, the word for wood (hout) takes the definite article "het" (het hout). The puzzle instructs them to take the letter O if the answer is "het hout".
Mission 3: The Starry Sky
Answer: U
Proof: An internet search for the Latin name of the Great Bear constellation reveals it is "Ursa Major". The first letter of the first word is U.
Mission 4: The English Journey
Answer: R
Proof: The river flowing past Big Ben in London is the River Thames. The first letter of the word River is R. (If they just look at Thames, hint 2 explicitly points them to the word "River").
Mission 5: The Gear Machine
Answer: A
Proof: Connected gears alternate directions. Gear 1 (Clockwise) -> Gear 2 (Counter-Clockwise) -> Gear 3 (Clockwise) -> Gear 4 (Counter-Clockwise). The puzzle assigns the letter A to Counter-Clockwise.
Mission 6: The Golden Weight
Answer: G
Proof: 50% + 40% = 90%. The remaining 10% is the head. 10% of 700 grams = 70 grams. The puzzle asks to divide this by 10 (70 ÷ 10 = 7). The 7th letter of the alphabet is G (A, B, C, D, E, F, G).
Mission 7: The Foreign Flag
Answer: E
Proof: The embedded image shows the flag of Greece. The 3rd letter of G-R-E-E-C-E is E.
🎉 Grand Finale Answer: C - O - U - R - A - G - E
The final reward value is COURAGE. The child successfully solved history, math, logic, language, and geography puzzles to activate the owl!
