The Museum Heist

Agent briefing — read carefully before you begin

A famous museum has found an empty frame in Gallery 7. The thief did not steal the painting for money. They left a trail of art clues to test whether a real detective can follow evidence, check facts, and think clearly.

Your job is to recover the curator's seal. Each answer gives you a clue. The final lock reveals the value that every great investigator needs.

MISSION 01
EASY

The Apron in the Frame

The thief first points you to Johannes Vermeer's painting The Milkmaid. Study the real painting image carefully.

What color is the large apron tied around the woman's waist?

Look at the cloth wrapped around her waist, not the cap or sleeves.
Hint 1

The answer is a color. Scan the lower middle of the painting.

MISSION 02
EASY

The Signature Search

The next clue says: "Find the huge painting called The Night Watch on the Rijksmuseum website."

Search the web for Rijksmuseum The Night Watch artist. What is the painter's surname?

Hint 1

Use the official Rijksmuseum result if you see it. The full artist name has three parts.

Did you know? The Rijksmuseum lists The Night Watch as 379.5 cm high and 453.5 cm wide.

MISSION 03
MEDIUM

The Frame Measure

The empty frame belongs to The Milkmaid. The Rijksmuseum lists its dimensions as height 45.5 cm and width 41 cm.

A rectangular frame needs two heights and two widths. What is the perimeter of the painting in centimeters?

Perimeter means the distance around the outside edge.
Hint 1

Add height + width + height + width.

Hint 2

Double 45.5, double 41, then add those two results.

MISSION 04
MEDIUM

The Dutch Label Mix-Up

The thief swapped four Dutch museum labels. Three use het. One uses de.

Which word is the odd one out?

het museum
het schilderij
het doek
de verf
Hint 1

You are not translating the words. You are comparing the article before each word.

Hint 2

Circle every word with het. The one left over is the answer.

Did you know? The Louvre says the Mona Lisa was stolen on 21 August 1911 and was missing for more than two years.

MISSION 05
HARD

The Guard Roster

Three guards were near Gallery 7: Noor, Bram, and Lina. One guard found the curator's seal and locked it in a safe.

Use the facts to find the guard.

1. The seal-finder was not carrying a flashlight.
2. Bram carried the radio.
3. The guard with the flashlight checked the west door.
4. Lina checked the east door.
5. Noor did not carry the keycard.

The three tools were flashlight, radio, and keycard. The three doors were west, east, and archive. Who found the seal?

Hint 1

Start with doors. If the flashlight guard checked the west door and Lina checked east, Lina did not have the flashlight.

Hint 2

Bram has the radio, so he cannot also have the flashlight or keycard. Work out who must have the flashlight, then remove that person.

MISSION 06
HARD

The Glass Case Fact-Check

The thief wrote: "The Mona Lisa is painted on canvas." A good detective checks claims before trusting them.

Search the Louvre page about the Mona Lisa in the Salle des Etats. What material does the Louvre say the Mona Lisa is painted on?

Hint 1

Look for the paragraph that explains why the painting is kept in a special glass case.

Hint 2

The answer is a type of wood, not a fabric.

Did you know? The Louvre keeps the Mona Lisa in a temperature and humidity-controlled glass case to help prevent more damage.

MISSION 07
BIG AHA

The Bedroom Inventory

The last gallery note mentions Vincent van Gogh's painting The Bedroom.

Search the Van Gogh Museum collection page for The Bedroom. The museum tags the work with several "More to explore" words. Which color appears directly after Yellow in that list?

Hint 1

Use the official Van Gogh Museum collection page, not a shopping page or poster page.

Hint 2

Find the "More to explore" list near the bottom of the page and read the colors in order.

GRAND FINALE
FINALE

The Curator's Cipher

You have checked images, facts, numbers, language, and logic. The thief left one final cipher on the back of the empty frame:

EJTDPWFSZ

Shift every letter one step backward in the alphabet. For example, B becomes A and F becomes E. What reward word does the cipher reveal?

Hint 1

Decode one letter at a time. Start with E.

Hint 2

The first four decoded letters should spell D I S C.

Case closed?

The museum seal can only be returned by an agent who follows the evidence: looking closely, checking sources, and changing their mind when the facts say so.
