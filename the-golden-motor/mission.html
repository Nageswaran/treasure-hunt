Mission File: The Secret of the Golden Motor

Welcome, Detective! We have an emergency. The blueprint for the world's most powerful, clean-energy motor has been hidden by a secret society of Dutch engineers. To prove you are clever enough to build it, they left behind a trail of puzzles. Grab your magnifying glass and fire up your search engine—it's time to build!

Mission 1: The Antique Prototype

Before you can build a new motor, you must understand the old ones. Look closely at this photograph of a classic electric motor used for science experiments.

Observation Check: Electricity flows into this motor through two round knobs (called terminals) at the very front of the wooden base. One knob is black. What color is the other knob?

💡 Hint 1

Look at the bottom section of the motor, closest to you. There are two plastic screws/knobs sticking up.

💡 Hint 2

The knob on the left is black. What color is the one right next to it on the right?

Mission 2: The Master Inventor

Did you know the electric car was invented long before gasoline cars? The first small-scale electric car was built right here in the Netherlands, in the city of Groningen, by a brilliant professor named Sibrandus Stratingh.

Open a new tab and search Google or Wikipedia for "Sibrandus Stratingh electric car". In what exact year did he build this first electric vehicle?

💡 Hint 1

The year is in the 1800s. It was a time when steam engines were popular.

Mission 3: The American Competitor

Around the same time, an American blacksmith named Thomas Davenport was building his own motors. Take a very close look at Davenport's 1837 motor below.

Look at the large wheel attached to the side. Does this wheel have thin metal spokes (like a bicycle wheel), or is it a completely solid disc?

💡 Hint 1

Zoom in on the wheel sitting on the right side of the wooden base.

Mission 4: The Dutch Blueprint Code

The combination to the blueprint safe is hidden inside the Dutch language. Find the correct letters from these engineering words to spell the answer (a famous unit of power)!

  1. The Dutch word for "wheel". Take the 1st letter.
  2. The Dutch word for a car "battery" (starts with A). Take the 1st letter.
  3. The Dutch word for electrical "current" (starts with S). Take the 2nd letter.
  4. The Dutch word for "motor". Take the 3rd letter.
💡 Hint 1

The words are: 1. Wiel 2. Accu 3. Stroom 4. Motor

Mission 5: Battery Mathematics

To power the new motor, you need the right amount of energy. Stratingh's small electric car weighed 3 kilograms, but batteries back then were very heavy!

If each battery cell weighs exactly 250 grams, and the engine requires exactly 4 cells to run, what is the TOTAL weight of the battery cells in grams? (Enter only the number).

💡 Hint 1

You need to multiply 250 by 4.

Mission 6: The Gear Logic

You find four mechanical gears on the workbench: a Copper gear, an Iron gear, a Brass gear, and a Steel gear. The instructions say you must use the gear on the far right.

Read the inventor's notes carefully to find their order:

  • The Copper gear is on the far left.
  • The Iron gear is sitting immediately to the right of the Copper gear.
  • The Brass gear is somewhere to the left of the Steel gear.

What material is the gear on the far right?

💡 Hint 1

Write down four blank spaces: _ _ _ _ . Since Copper is on the far left, it goes in space 1. Iron is right next to it, so Iron is in space 2.

💡 Hint 2

You only have spaces 3 and 4 left. Brass must be left of Steel. Where do they go?

Look carefully at the large building in the photo. What color are the roof tiles covering the top of the house?
