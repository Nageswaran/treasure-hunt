Mission 01 · Easy

The Green-Faced Guardian

Answer: green
How to get there

Look at the photo. The skin of the face is painted green, while the headdress is striped blue and gold/cream.

Why this is the only answer

The clue specifically asks about the face's skin color, not the headdress or collar — the face is unambiguously green in the photo.

Source / fact used

Real photo of the coffin of Peftjaoeneith from the Rijksmuseum van Oudheden's Egyptian collection, via Wikimedia Commons. Green face paint on Egyptian coffins and mummy masks symbolized rebirth, associated with the god Osiris, who was traditionally depicted with green skin (confirmed via web search of Egyptology sources, e.g. discussions of Osiris iconography and mummy mask symbolism).

Mission 02 · Easy

The Rising Water

Answer: the Aswan (High) Dam
How to get there

Searching "Temple of Taffeh dam" leads directly to the story of the Aswan High Dam, built across the Nile in Egypt, whose reservoir threatened to permanently flood ancient Nubian monuments including the Temple of Taffeh.

Why this is the only answer

Every source describing why the temple was moved names the same dam; this fact is consistent across the Wikipedia article, the Rijksmuseum van Oudheden's own page, and multiple news sources.

Source / fact used

Wikipedia: Temple of Taffeh; Rijksmuseum van Oudheden collection page.

Mission 03 · Medium

The Museum Labels

Answer: graf and masker
How to get there

The correct Dutch articles are: de mummie, het graf, de amulet, het masker, de tempel, de sarcofaag. The two "het" words are graf and masker.

Why this is the only answer

Dutch grammatical gender for these words is fixed and verified against Dutch dictionaries; there is no valid alternative article for any of the six words.

Source / fact used

Verified via Dutch lidwoord reference sites (welklidwoord.nl, woorden.org, webwoordenboek.nl) for each individual word.

Mission 04 · Medium

The Lake That Almost Won

Answer: Lake Nasser
How to get there

The Aswan High Dam (found in Mission 02) created a huge reservoir called Lake Nasser, named after Gamal Abdel Nasser, the Egyptian president during construction. Rising waters from this lake threatened dozens of ancient monuments.

Why this is the only answer

Lake Nasser is the universally used name for this reservoir in every source; there is no competing name.

Source / fact used

Wikipedia: Temple of Taffeh (UNESCO Nubia rescue campaign).

Mission 05 · Hard

The Combination Lock

Answer: 618
How to get there

The temple was rebuilt from 657 stone blocks. It was built between 25 BCE and 14 CE, which spans 25 + 14 = 39 years (no year zero exists between 1 BCE and 1 CE). 657 − 39 = 618.

Why this is the only answer

Both the block count (657) and the construction date range (25 BCE–14 CE) are stated consistently across independent sources, so the subtraction yields one fixed number.

Source / fact used

Wikipedia: Temple of Taffeh — "constructed from 657 blocks weighing approximately 250 tons," built 25 BCE–14 CE under Augustus.

Mission 07 · Big Aha

The Detective Academy Code

Answer: THE CURSE IS ONLY A TEST
How to get there

Shift each letter of "WKH FXUVH LV RQOB D WHVW" backward by 3: W→T, K→H, H→E; F→C, X→U, U→R, V→S, H→E; L→I, V→S; R→O, Q→N, O→L, B→Y; D→A; W→T, H→E, V→S, W→T. Result: THE CURSE IS ONLY A TEST.

Why this is the only answer

A Caesar shift cipher has exactly one decoding for a fixed shift amount; shifting each letter back by 3 gives one unique result.
