Internet Detective Academy · Case File

The Mummy's Warning

Agent briefing — read carefully before you begin

The Rijksmuseum van Oudheden in Leiden, the Netherlands, is home to a real ancient Egyptian temple and a gallery full of real mummies. This morning, staff arrived to find one of the oldest display cases standing open — and a strange note written in code left on the floor, warning of an ancient "curse."

Visitors return this afternoon. You've been called in to investigate every clue before the doors open, and to find out whether this "curse" is anything to actually worry about.

MISSION 01
EASY

The Green-Faced Guardian

Detectives, meet a real witness of sorts. Below is a photograph of an actual ancient Egyptian coffin from the museum's own Egyptian gallery — more than 2,500 years old. Egyptian artists never picked their paint colors by accident.

Look closely at the face on the coffin. What single color was it painted?

Look only at the skin of the face itself, not the striped headdress.
Hint 1

It's the same color as fresh plants growing along the Nile river.

MISSION 02
EASY

The Rising Water

This temple wasn't built in the Netherlands. It was carefully taken apart, shipped all the way from Egypt, and rebuilt here — because a huge modern construction project in Egypt threatened to flood it forever.

Search online to find the name of the famous dam, built across the river Nile in Egypt in the 20th century, that caused this flooding threat.

Hint 1

Try searching "Temple of Taffeh" together with the word "dam".

Did you know? Ancient Egyptians often painted the faces on mummy masks and coffins green — the color of plants along the Nile — because green stood for rebirth and new life after death.

MISSION 03
MEDIUM

The Museum Labels

Every object in this Dutch museum has a small label written in Dutch. In Dutch, some words use de and some use het — never both, and there's no simple rule for which is which. Detectives-in-training just have to know them.

mummie
graf
amulet
masker
tempel
sarcofaag

Four of these six words use de. Which two words use het?

Hint 1

Try saying "het" in front of each word out loud. If it sounds wrong to a Dutch ear, it's probably a de-word instead.

Hint 2

One of the het-words rhymes with "straf". The other one covers your face at Carnaval.

MISSION 04
MEDIUM

The Lake That Almost Won

That dam from Mission 02 didn't just make electricity — it also created an enormous new lake that swallowed up whole villages and ancient monuments across the desert.

Search to find the name of this giant man-made lake.

Hint 1

The lake is named after a famous Egyptian president from the 1950s and 60s.

Hint 2

Search "Aswan Dam" plus the word "lake" and look for the lake's proper name.

Did you know? The whole Temple of Taffeh was taken apart into 657 separate stone blocks and shipped from Egypt to the Netherlands, then rebuilt piece by piece — like the world's heaviest jigsaw puzzle.

MISSION 05
HARD

The Combination Lock

The museum's old archive cabinet has a combination lock. To crack it, detectives must calculate:

[the number of stone blocks used to rebuild the whole Temple of Taffeh]
MINUS
[the number of years it took to build the temple, from 25 BCE to 14 CE]

Search to find the number of blocks, then do the math. Remember: history has no "year zero" between BCE and CE — the year 1 BCE is immediately followed by the year 1 CE.

Hint 1

Search "Temple of Taffeh" together with the word "blocks" to find how many separate stones make up the whole temple.

Hint 2

From 25 BCE to 14 CE is 25 + 14 years, not 25 + 14 − 1, because there is no year 0 to subtract.

MISSION 06
HARD

Who Left the Case Open?

Four staff members had access to the Egyptian gallery yesterday: Anna, Ben, Cato, and Daan. One of them accidentally left the display case unlocked. Use these facts to find out who:

1. Whoever left the case unlocked was working in the Egypt gallery after 6 PM.
2. Anna and Daan both signed out and went home before 5 PM.
3. Only two staff members stayed after 6 PM. One of them was polishing the glass display cases in the gallery. The other stayed in the office doing paperwork and never once entered the gallery.
4. Cato spent the whole evening in the office doing paperwork.

Who left the case unlocked?

Hint 1

Start by ruling out who was definitely NOT in the building after 6 PM.

Hint 2

If only two people stayed after 6 PM, and you already know exactly what one of them was doing all night, the other one only has one job left.

Did you know? The museum's Egyptian gallery displays more than 1,400 real ancient objects — and scientists scanned the mummies with medical scanners so they could see what's inside without ever unwrapping them.

MISSION 07
BIG AHA

The Detective Academy Code

Tucked inside the empty case, investigators found this coded note — written in the same secret shift-code every Detective Academy trainee learns in their first week. Each real letter was shifted forward by 3 places in the alphabet (A becomes D, B becomes E, and so on).

WKH FXUVH LV RQOB D WHVW

Shift every letter backward by 3 places to read the true message. What does it say?

Hint 1

To go backward, count 3 letters earlier in the alphabet for every single letter: W back 3 is T, K back 3 is H...

Hint 2

Leave the spaces exactly where they are — they show you where each word ends. The first word is three letters long.

GRAND FINALE
FINALE

The Real Treasure

Case closed, detective! One last message waits in the museum director's note, written in the very same shift-3 code you just cracked. Decode it to discover the true reward for solving "The Mummy's Warning":

SHUVHYHUDQFH
Hint 1

Same trick as before — shift every letter backward by 3 places in the alphabet.

Hint 2

It's a single English word, 12 letters long, that describes never giving up — even when a job takes years and years, like moving an entire stone temple block by block.

Case closed?
