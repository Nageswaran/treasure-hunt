Internet Detective Academy · Wildlife Unit · Case File

The Zoo's Missing Red Panda

Agent briefing — read carefully before you begin
⏱ Estimated case time: 50–70 minutes
This morning the City Zoo opened to a shock: Fenna the red panda was not in her enclosure! The gate was open, but nothing was broken. The keepers are worried someone took her. As the Academy's Wildlife Detective, it's your job to follow the clues, learn about red pandas, avoid a nasty online trick, and figure out what really happened to Fenna — and to end the case with the one thing every animal needs most.
MISSION 01
EASY

Follow the Paw Prints

Fenna left a trail of muddy paw prints leading away from her open gate and across the grass. If you can count them, you'll know she walked off on her own four feet — she wasn't carried.

Look at the picture. How many red-panda paw prints are in the trail? (Type just the number.)

Each paw print has one big pad with four little toe-dots above it. Count the whole prints.
Hint 1

Start at the open gate on the left and follow the trail toward the tree on the right. Touch each paw print once as you count. They lead up and across, so don't miss the ones higher up.

MISSION 02
EASY

Where Do Red Pandas Come From?

To find Fenna, it helps to know her kind. In the wild, red pandas live high up in cool mountain forests in countries like Nepal, Bhutan, India, China and Myanmar.

Your internet task: Search for "where do red pandas live" and find out which continent all of those countries belong to. Type the name of the continent.

Hint 1

Search: which continent is Nepal in. All five countries — Nepal, Bhutan, India, China and Myanmar — are on the same, very large continent, and its name begins with "A".

💡 Did you know? A red panda has a long, bushy, striped tail that it wraps around its body like a cosy blanket to stay warm on cold mountain nights.

MISSION 03
MEDIUM

The Zoo's Dutch Animal Signs

The City Zoo is in the Netherlands, so every enclosure has a Dutch name-sign. Here are four animal words. Three use the article "de". Only one uses "het".

The words are: olifant (elephant), tijger (tiger), panda (panda), konijn (rabbit).

Which single word goes with "het"? (Type the Dutch word.)

Hint 1

Say each word with "de" and then with "het". Three of them sound right with "de". The odd one out is the small, long-eared, hopping animal.

MISSION 04
MEDIUM

The Ticket-Booth Sum

While you investigate, a family arrives at the zoo ticket booth to help with the search. The prices are:

🎟️ Child ticket: €8  •  🎟️ Adult ticket: €12

The family buys 2 adult tickets and 3 child tickets. What is the total they pay, in euros? (Type just the number.)

Hint 1

Work out the two groups on their own first: 2 adults at €12 each, and 3 children at €8 each. Then add your two answers together.

💡 Did you know? Red pandas are such amazing climbers that they can walk down a tree trunk head-first — something almost no other animal can do, thanks to ankles that bend right around.

MISSION 05
HARD

What Does Fenna Eat?

To coax Fenna back safely, the keepers need her favourite food. Red pandas are picky eaters — almost their whole diet is just one single plant, the same tall, green, hollow plant that giant pandas love too.

Your internet task: Search for "what do red pandas mostly eat". What is the one plant that makes up about 95% of a red panda's food? (Type the plant's name.)

Hint 1

Search: red panda diet. It's the same fast-growing plant that giant pandas eat, and its name begins with "b".

Hint 2

It's a tall grass with hollow, jointed stems that grows in Asian forests. Pandas — both red and giant — munch it all day long.

💡 Did you know? Red pandas spend so much time nibbling that one red panda can eat around 20,000 bamboo leaves in a single day!

MISSION 06
HARD

The "Free Red Panda" Trap

While searching, the zoo's computer gets this pop-up message:

🎉 CONGRATULATIONS!!! You have WON a FREE baby red panda!!! Click this link and type your password RIGHT NOW to claim your prize before it's gone!!! ⏰

This is a classic online scam — a trick to steal something. A real zoo would never message you like this. Tip: you can even search for the zoo's real website to compare — the real one never asks for this.

Question: There is one secret thing you must never type or share with a stranger online to claim a "prize." In one word, what is that secret thing the scam is trying to steal?

Hint 1

Look closely at what the message is actually asking you to type in. It is the secret word you use to log in to an account — something you should keep private and never give away.

MISSION 07
AHA!

Who Saw Fenna Last?

Three keepers were on duty this morning: Nina, Ravi and Bo. Each one looked after a different animal: the penguins, the lions, or the red panda. Whoever looked after the red panda was the last person to see Fenna — and might know where she went. Work out who that was.

Clue 1: Nina spent the whole morning at the penguin pool.
Clue 2: Ravi is a little scared of the big cats, so he did not look after the lions.
Clue 3: Each keeper looked after just one, different animal.

Who looked after the red panda? (Type the keeper's first name.)

Hint 1

Clue 1 puts Nina with the penguins. So the red panda and the lions belong to Ravi and Bo. Now use Clue 2 to decide which is which.

Hint 2

Ravi didn't take the lions, and Nina had the penguins — so Ravi must have looked after the red panda, and Bo took the lions.

GRAND FINALE
FINALE

Bringing Fenna Home

Ravi remembered the truth: no one stole Fenna at all. Startled by a loud rubbish truck, she had slipped through the gate a keeper forgot to latch, and climbed high into the big oak tree by the café — right where her paw prints led.

Now comes the most important part. You don't chase or grab a frightened animal. You stay calm and quiet, set out some bamboo, give her space, and gently wait. That gentle, caring way of treating every creature has a name — and it's the reward of this whole case.

Unscramble these eight letters to find it:

S S E N D N I K

Type the 8-letter word that means "being warm, gentle and caring toward others."

Hint 1

The word starts with "K". It's how you treated Fenna — calm, gentle and caring — instead of scaring her.

Hint 2

It begins K-I-N-D… and then "-ness". Put those together.

Case closed?

Fenna climbed down on her own and padded straight to the bamboo you left out. Case solved — and no one ever had to frighten her. The reward of this case is KINDNESS: you used your head to find the facts, and your heart to bring Fenna home safely. That's what makes a truly great Wildlife Detective. 🐾
