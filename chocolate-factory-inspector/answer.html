Mission 04 · Medium

Checking the Rules

Answer: 35
How to get there

Search "minimum cocoa percentage dark chocolate rule" or check Wikipedia's "Types of chocolate" article. Both the EU standard and the general international/Codex/US guidance agree: dark chocolate must contain at least 35% total dry cocoa solids (including at least 18% cocoa butter and 14% dry non-fat cocoa solids under the EU rule).

Why this is the only answer

35% is the consistent baseline figure across the EU legal definition, the general Codex/US guidance, and multiple chocolate-industry explainer sites — no other number is given as the minimum for the "dark chocolate" category.

Source / fact used

Verified via web search: EU chocolate labeling rules and Wikipedia's "Types of chocolate" article, both stating a 35% minimum total cocoa solids requirement for dark chocolate.

Mission 06 · Hard

The Name on the Wall

Answer: Van Houten
How to get there

Search "1828 cocoa press invented" or look up "Dutch process cocoa" / "Coenraad Johannes van Houten" on Wikipedia or Britannica. Both sources credit the Van Houten family (Britannica: "C. J. van Houten... patented a process for pressing much of the fat... from ground and roasted cocoa beans") with the 1828 hydraulic cocoa press that made cocoa powder possible — the process is still called "Dutch process" cocoa today.

Why this is the only answer

Every mainstream source (Wikipedia, Britannica, the Van Houten brand's own history page) uses the surname "Van Houten" for this 1828 invention. There is a minor historical footnote that the patent may actually trace to Coenraad's father Casparus van Houten — but both share the same family surname, so "Van Houten" is correct regardless of which reading a child's search turns up.

Source / fact used

Verified via web search: Britannica ("C. J. van Houten | Dutch manufacturer"), Wikipedia ("Coenraad Johannes van Houten" and "Dutch process cocoa").
