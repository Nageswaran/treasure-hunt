🔑 Parent & Teacher Answer Key

Case File: The Mystery of the Maharaja's Spice Map (v3.0)

Use this guide to support your child as they navigate the investigation. Encourage them to verify their answers independently before checking here!

✅ Mission 1: The Spice Market Scan

Correct Answer: 5

Explanation: Looking carefully at the front row resting directly on the red tablecloth surface, there are exactly 5 blue bowls containing green, orange, yellow, dark red, and light grey spices. Other containers in the background are metallic or hanging bags.

Skill Developed: Visual inspection, sorting foreground objects from chaotic backgrounds.

Common Mistake: Children might count the silver spoons or look at the hanging bags behind the main table. Guide them to look only at the bottom-most row of bowls.

✅ Mission 2: Tracking the Capital

Correct Answer: New Delhi

Explanation: A direct search for "capital of India" points explicitly to New Delhi (distinct from Old Delhi or Mumbai).

Skill Developed: Direct query searching and recognizing official modern geographic capitals.

✅ Mission 3: Guard of the Jungle

Correct Answer: Bengal Tiger

Explanation: The picture shows *Panthera tigris tigris*, commonly known as the Bengal Tiger, which is native to the Indian subcontinent.

Skill Developed: Information retrieval via animal physical characteristics.

✅ Mission 4: The Dutch Baker's Note

Correct Answer: bak

Explanation: In Dutch primary school grammar (Groep 6 level), the 'stam' of a verb is the root form, generally found by taking the infinitive minus '-en' (with minor adjustments for vowel length if needed). For *bakken*, stripping the '-en' and eliminating the double consonant leaves **bak** (which matches the first-person singular 'ik-vorm').

Skill Developed: Applying Dutch spelling rules and morphological verb analysis.

✅ Mission 5: Quick-Learn Road Safety Sign

Correct Answer: B) Children playing or crossing ahead

Explanation: Traffic sign J22 in the Netherlands depicts two running silhouettes inside a red warning triangle, alerting drivers to the potential presence of children in nearby residential or school zones.

Skill Developed: Practical interpretation of public visual infrastructure and safety guidelines.

✅ Mission 6: Real-World Scenario (The Recipe Secret)

Correct Answer: B

Explanation: Option B is the only safe choice. This puzzle acts as a lesson in digital safety and integrity, instructing kids never to trade confidential information or fulfill requests from strangers online in exchange for physical rewards.

Skill Developed: Online boundary setting, risk aversion, and open communication with parents.

✅ Mission 7: Breaking the Final Code Word

Correct Answer: NBB

Explanation:
• Mission 2 Capital = New Delhi
• Mission 3 Animal = Bengal Tiger
• Mission 4 Verb Root = Bak
Combining these gives the password code NBB.

💡 Coaching Tip for Parents

If the child gets stuck on the final code word, check that they didn't write "Delhi" instead of "New Delhi" for Mission 2, or "Tiger" instead of "Bengal Tiger" for Mission 3, as this will change their starting letter combinations. Emphasize that precision is the secret to a great detective's success!
