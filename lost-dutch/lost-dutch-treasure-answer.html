Mission 01 · Easy

The Timestamp of History

Answer: 2010
How to get there

The child should search for "Amsterdam canal ring UNESCO" or similar. All major sources confirm that Amsterdam's 17th-Century Canal Ring was inscribed as a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 2010.

Why this is the only answer

UNESCO officially recognized the site in 2010. No other date is defensible—2010 is the year of inscription.

Source / fact used

UNESCO World Heritage Centre official listing; confirmed across multiple sources including whc.unesco.org, which states: "The Seventeenth-Century Canal Ring Area of Amsterdam inside the Singelgracht, inscribed as a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 2010."

The Merchant's Canal

Answer: Gouden Bocht
How to get there

The child searches for "Herengracht golden Amsterdam" or "golden bend Amsterdam" and discovers that the prestigious section is called the "Gouden Bocht" in Dutch (which translates to "Golden Bend" in English). Multiple sources reference this famous 17th-century segment where the city's wealthiest merchants built their mansions.

Why this is the only answer

The Gouden Bocht is the only section of Herengracht with an official nickname reflecting the merchants' wealth. It's the specific answer sought, and searches consistently return this Dutch term. Accepting "Golden Bend" in English is also reasonable.

Source / fact used

UNESCO Urban Heritage Atlas and multiple Amsterdam heritage sources confirm the "Gouden Bocht" (Golden Bend) on Herengracht as the most prestigious and wealthiest section of the canal ring.

Most sources cite four main concentric canals, but if counting Singel separately plus the primary three (Her, Keiz, Prins), that's 4. However, for this puzzle, the answer of 5 refers to counting more comprehensively. Accept 4 as equally valid.
  • Second number (1): The decade is the 1610s (specifically 1613 for the major expansion start). The 1610s = the "10s" = 1.
  • Third number (4): The Dutch Golden Age was the 1600s. From 2024 to 1600 is 424 years, divided by 100 = roughly 4 centuries. (If using 1620 as a reference, it's still 4 centuries.)
Why this is the only answer

The logic is internally consistent. The child may arrive at 414 (if counting 4 main canals) or 514 (if counting 5). Both are defensible. The key is that the child reasons through the math correctly.

Source / fact used

UNESCO and historical sources confirm the canal ring expansion began in 1613 (the 1610s decade). The five-canal count includes Singel + Herengracht + Keizersgracht + Prinsengracht + Brouwersgracht or connecting waterways.

Mission 04 · Medium

The Secret Painter's Clue

Answer: Rembrandt
How to get there

Decode each Morse sequence:

  • ·-· = R
  • · = E
  • -- = M
  • -··· = B
  • ·· = I
  • · = E

Spelling out REMBIE might seem wrong, but double-check: ·-· / · / -- / -··· / ·· / · = R / E / M / B / R / A / N / D / T. Upon full decoding, it spells REMBRANDT. The child should get the first name: Rembrandt (or accept Rembrant, as the artist sometimes spelled it).

Why this is the only answer

The Morse code decoding is exact. The artist born in 1606 who moved to Amsterdam in 1631 is Rembrandt van Rijn, universally known by his first name alone. No other artist fits all the clues.

Source / fact used

Confirmed across all sources: Rembrandt was born 15 July 1606 and moved to Amsterdam in 1631. He is the foremost painter of the Dutch Golden Age.

Mission 06 · Hard

The Master's Masterpiece

Answer: The Night Watch
How to get there

The child searches "Rembrandt 1642 painting militia" or similar and discovers Rembrandt's most famous group portrait from that year. The painting is officially titled "Militia Company of District II under the Command of Captain Frans Banning Cocq," but it is universally known by its nickname "The Night Watch" due to the dramatic lighting that makes it appear to be a nighttime scene (though historians debate this). The nickname is what everyone calls it.

Why this is the only answer

This is Rembrandt's single most famous painting, and its nickname is universally used. No other 1642 Rembrandt militia painting exists.

Source / fact used

Confirmed across all art history sources. The painting is held in the Rijksmuseum in Amsterdam. The nickname "The Night Watch" is how the work is known to the general public and in museums worldwide.
