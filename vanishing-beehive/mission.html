Internet Detective Academy · Case File

The Case of the Vanishing Bees

Agent briefing — read carefully before you begin
⏱ Estimated case time: 50-70 minutes

Yesterday morning, the beekeeper at Willowbrook Research Apiary opened Hive C and found it almost empty. No dead bees anywhere — just the queen's old comb and a lot of quiet. Online, some people are calling it "Colony Collapse Disorder." Others think something simpler happened. The apiary needs a detective who can tell fact from guesswork. That detective is you.

MISSION 01
EASY

The Watchful Insect

The beekeeper's notebook has a riddle taped to the first page: "I have far more eyes than you'd think — some for colors, some just for light." Search online to find out exactly how many eyes a honeybee has in total (counting both the big eyes and the tiny extra ones on top of its head).

Hint 1

Bees have two kinds of eyes: large compound eyes on the sides, and small simple eyes (called ocelli) on top. Search for both kinds and add them together.

💡 Did you know? A honeybee has 5 eyes in total: 2 large compound eyes for seeing colors and shapes, and 3 tiny simple eyes on top of its head that only sense light and direction.

MISSION 02
EASY

The Honeycomb Frame

Inside Hive C, one frame of honeycomb was left behind. Some of its hexagon cells still have a golden wax cap sealing the honey inside — the rest are empty. Count only the capped, golden cells.

Golden cells are capped and full of honey. Grey outlined cells are empty.
Hint 1

Look only at the middle row — how many of those cells are colored gold?

MISSION 03
MEDIUM

The Hourly Count

A research camera counted bees flying past a sensor near the hive entrance every hour, before the swarm left. The counts followed a pattern: 3, 6, 12, 24, ... If the pattern continued exactly the same way, what number would the sensor have shown on the 6th hour?

Hint 1

Look at what happens from one number to the next. Once you spot the rule, apply it twice more to reach the 6th hour.

MISSION 04
MEDIUM

The Beekeeper's Word List

The beekeeper's notebook lists five bee-related words. Four of them use "de" and one uses "het". Find the odd one out.

honingbij · korf · zwerm · imker · nest

Hint 1

Search "de of het" plus each word to check its article. Four words share the same one.

💡 Did you know? Bees build their honeycomb cells as hexagons because that shape packs together perfectly with no wasted space, storing the most honey while using the least amount of wax.

MISSION 05
HARD

Three Assistants, One Truth

Three research assistants each guess which hive (A, B, or C) the missing bees actually moved into. Only one of the three statements below is true. Which hive is it?

Elin says: "It's Hive A or Hive B."
Farah says: "It's not Hive A."
Gido says: "It's Hive C."

Hint 1

Try each hive one at a time. For each guess, check how many of the three statements would be true — only one hive makes exactly one statement true.

Hint 2

If the hive were B, both Elin's and Farah's statements would be true at once — too many. Try A instead.

MISSION 06
HARD

Colony Collapse — or Something Else?

Search online to compare two real bee phenomena: "colony collapse disorder" and "honeybee swarming". Then look at the evidence from Hive A: no dead bees anywhere, a large cluster of bees was seen clinging to a tree branch nearby that same morning, and it happened in early summer. Which phenomenon actually matches this evidence?

Hint 1

One of the two phenomena is known for leaving a visible cluster of living bees (with the queen) on a nearby branch or structure. The other does not.

💡 Did you know? A queen bee can live 3 to 5 years — sometimes even longer — while the worker bees in her own hive usually live only 4 to 6 weeks each during summer.

MISSION 07
BIG AHA!

The Logbook Lock

The apiary's old logbook is locked with a 3-character code: one letter followed by two digits. Build it from what you've already found, in this order:

Character 1: the letter of the hive the bees really moved into (Mission 5).
Characters 2-3: the number of eyes a honeybee has (Mission 1), followed by the number of capped honeycomb cells (Mission 2).

Put them together with no spaces. What's the code?

Hint 1

Line up your three earlier answers in the exact order listed, letter first, then the two digits.

GRAND FINALE
FINALE

The Beekeeper's Last Note

The logbook opens to one final page: a message in Morse code. Decode it to find the word the whole apiary lives by — the reason a swarm of thousands of bees can act like one.

Search for a Morse code chart and decode one letter at a time, in order.

The first letter (a single dash) is T. The word has 8 letters.

Case closed?

The bees weren't lost at all — they were doing exactly what healthy colonies do, together. No single bee could have built a new home alone. The real discovery was teamwork: thousands of tiny decisions, made as one. Well done, Agent.
