Internet Detective Academy · Case File

The Sunken Ship's Secret Cargo

Agent briefing — read carefully before you begin
⏱ Estimated case time: 55-75 minutes

A museum archive just recovered a water-damaged logbook from the wreck of the De Adelaar, a 17th-century trading ship that sank on its way home from the Spice Islands. Most pages are unreadable — but a few survived, coded in the captain's own hand. Historians think the ship's final cargo is still down there, exactly where the log says. Follow the clues, and you could be the first to pinpoint it in almost 400 years.

MISSION 01
EASY

The Company Behind the Ship

A faded stamp on the logbook's cover reads "VOC" — the initials of the Dutch East India Company, the trading company that owned ships like this one. Search online to find the exact year this company was founded.

Hint 1

Search "Dutch East India Company founded year" or look up "VOC 1602" to check the date.

💡 Did you know? The VOC's 1602 share offering was the world's first initial public offering (IPO). For the first time ever, ordinary people could buy a piece of a company on the world's first stock exchange, in Amsterdam.

MISSION 02
EASY

The Odd Seal

Six crates from the cargo hold were recovered, each with a wax seal stamped on its lid. Five crates share the exact same seal. One doesn't match. Which crate number is it?

Compare every seal carefully — five have a small diamond shape inside the circle, one is just a plain circle.
Hint 1

Go crate by crate, left to right, and check whether you can see a diamond shape inside the circle.

MISSION 03
MEDIUM

The Captain's Ledger

One surviving ledger page records a single trade: "Bought 1 sack of nutmeg in the Indies for 40 guilders. Sold the same sack in Amsterdam for 200 guilders." What percent profit did the captain make on this one trade?

Hint 1

First find the profit in guilders (selling price minus buying price). Then compare that profit to the original 40 guilders, as a percentage.

💡 Did you know? Nutmeg and cloves were once worth more than gold and silver in Europe. In the Middle Ages, a single nutmeg could be worth enough to buy a house, because of how far it had to travel.

MISSION 04
MEDIUM

Islands of Fortune

The nutmeg aboard the De Adelaar came from a real group of islands in present-day Indonesia, called the Maluku Islands. Search to find the famous nickname these islands share, earned because of the valuable spices that grow there.

Hint 1

Search "Maluku Islands nickname" — the nickname is two words, and it's exactly what you'd expect from an island full of nutmeg and cloves.

MISSION 05
HARD

The Mirror Alphabet

One coded strip uses a mirror trick: A becomes Z, B becomes Y, C becomes X — each letter swaps with its opposite-end partner all the way through the alphabet. Decode this word. It's the compass direction the wreck lies in from the last known port.

HLFGSVZHG

Hint 1

Write the alphabet forwards on one line and backwards underneath it (A under Z, B under Y...), then match each coded letter to the one above or below it.

Hint 2

The first letter, H, swaps with S. The word has 9 letters and names a direction between two compass points.

MISSION 06
HARD

The Captain's Crate Numbers

The captain numbered his crates in a pattern, skipping numbers on purpose so rival sailors couldn't guess how many crates he had: 2, 5, 11, 23, 47, ... If he kept numbering the same way, what number would the next crate have?

Hint 1

Look at what you do to each number to get the next one. It's a two-step rule, not just adding or just multiplying.

Hint 2

Try doubling a number, then see what small adjustment gets you to the next one in the list.

💡 Did you know? By the time it stopped trading, the VOC had operated more than 1,500 ships and employed over a million people across Europe and Asia — making it one of the largest companies the world has ever seen.

MISSION 07
BIG AHA!

The Sea Chest's Dial

A small iron chest has a 4-dial combination lock. Build the code from what you've already found, in this exact order:

Dial 1-2: the last two digits of the year the VOC was founded (Mission 1).
Dial 3: the number of the odd crate (Mission 2).
Dial 4: the first digit of the next crate number in the captain's pattern (Mission 6).

Put all four digits together, in order, with no spaces (keep any leading zero). What's the code?

Hint 1

1602's last two digits are "02" — don't drop the leading zero. Then add your Mission 2 and Mission 6 digits in order.

GRAND FINALE
FINALE

The Captain's Final Word

The chest creaks open at last. Inside, instead of gold, is a single folded note in a shifted code: every letter was moved forward by 5 in the alphabet (A→F, B→G, and so on, wrapping around after Z). Shift each letter back by 5 to read the captain's last word.

BNXITR

Hint 1

Count backwards 5 letters in the alphabet from each coded letter, one at a time.

Hint 2

The first letter, B, shifted back 5 becomes W. The word has 6 letters.

Case closed?

The chest held no gold — just the captain's careful notes, kept for the historians who'd one day come looking. Following his ciphers, his numbers, and his ledger taught you more than any treasure could. That's wisdom: knowledge earned by working for it. Well done, Agent.
