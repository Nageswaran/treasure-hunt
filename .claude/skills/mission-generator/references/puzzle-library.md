# Puzzle Library

## Story themes

Pick one per mission set, or invent a new one in the same spirit. Check the conversation for themes already used and skip those.

- The Lost Dutch Treasure
- The Mystery of the Golden Windmill
- The Secret Time Machine
- The Missing Scientist
- The AI Robot That Escaped
- The Hidden City Under Amsterdam
- The Forgotten Castle
- The Pirate's Secret Map
- The Space Explorer's Code
- The Museum Heist
- The Ancient Egyptian Curse

In the same spirit, you can also invent fresh ones (a missing-letter mystery, a wildlife-smuggling case at a zoo, a sunken-ship expedition, a code hidden in a famous painting, a runaway weather balloon, a school-mascot kidnapping...). Match the theme to any subject the user requested (e.g. "she loves space and the ocean" → a deep-sea or astronaut mystery).

## Puzzle mechanics

Mix and match across the mission set — avoid leaning on the same mechanic for more than two missions in a row.

- Internet research (search → find a specific fact)
- Google Maps / Street View investigation
- Hidden clues in an image (zoom in, spot the detail)
- Secret/coded messages
- Spot the difference (between two images, or two versions of a fact)
- Riddles
- Mini logic games
- Decoding puzzles (cipher, binary, Morse)
- Mathematical treasure hunts (the math answer is a coordinate, a page number, a combination, etc.)
- Combining clues from multiple sources/websites
- Virtual "escape-room inventory" (collect clue-items across missions, combine them at the end)
- Branching clues (the answer to one mission picks which path/clue comes next)
- Comparing sources (which of two claims/sites is actually correct)
- Finding the mistake (in a sentence, image, or claim)
- Multi-step reasoning chains (clue A unlocks clue B unlocks the answer)

## "Did you know?" facts — examples of the right shape

These are *examples of style*, not facts to reuse without checking — always verify with `web_search` before using any fact for real, since these may drift out of date or be misremembered.

- Did you know the Netherlands has more bicycles than people?
- Did you know prime numbers can only be divided evenly by 1 and themselves?
- Did you know octopuses have three hearts?
- Did you know Mount Everest grows a few millimeters taller every year?
- Did you know honey never spoils if it's sealed properly?

## Reward values

The final mission's answer should reveal one of these:

curiosity · courage · discovery · creativity · teamwork · kindness · wisdom · perseverance

Pick whichever fits the story arc best (e.g. a mystery solved through persistence → perseverance; a mission where the child had to combine very different clue types → creativity).
